Do's:

Audit your privacy settings: Regularly check who can see your posts, photos, and personal details across all platforms.

Showcase your projects and skills: Use platforms like LinkedIn or GitHub to highlight your coding projects, academic achievements, and professional growth.

Think before you post: Apply the "Billboard Rule"—if you wouldn't want it plastered on a billboard for everyone to see, don't post it.

Engage respectfully: Build a supportive network by participating in discussions constructively and cheering on your peers' accomplishments.

Set boundaries for screen time: Use app limits or schedule specific times to check social media so it doesn't interfere with your coursework and sleep.

 Don'ts:
Don't post anything you wouldn't want an employer to see: Avoid sharing photos or content involving illegal activities or unprofessional behavior.

Don't complain publicly about your university or professors: Venting online can easily be screenshotted and shared, damaging your academic reputation.

Don't overshare sensitive personal information: Keep your exact address, class schedule, student ID number, and real-time location private for safety reasons.

Don't engage in internet drama or cyberbullying: Avoid getting dragged into heated, public arguments that leave a permanent negative trail.

Don't rely on social media as your only news source: Always verify information through credible, objective sources before sharing it with your network.
