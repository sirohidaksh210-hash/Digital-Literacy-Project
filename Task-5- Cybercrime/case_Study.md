### Case Study: The "Payment Request" Trap

**What is the crime?** UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of financial cybercrime where attackers trick victims into authorizing debit transactions from their bank accounts using mobile payment apps.

**Step-by-Step Scenario:**
1. **The Hook:** A student lists a used smartphone for sale on a platform like OLX. A "buyer" contacts them immediately, agreeing to the full price without negotiation to build excitement.
2. **The Deception:** The scammer claims they are busy and will pay via UPI. They send a "Payment Request" or a QR code to the student via WhatsApp, claiming, "Scan this and enter your PIN to receive the money."
3. **The Trap:** The student, unaware that a PIN is **never** required to receive money, scans the code and enters their secret 4 or 6-digit UPI PIN.
4. **The Theft:** Instead of receiving funds, the student’s account is instantly debited. The scammer immediately blocks the student on all platforms.

**Target Audience:** College students and young adults in India are primary targets due to their high frequency of online shopping, peer-to-peer selling, and heavy reliance on digital wallets.

**Consequences:** Victims face immediate financial loss, often involving semester fees or monthly allowances. Beyond the money, it leads to severe psychological stress, "tech-anxiety," and potential compromise of linked bank account details.

## Part B: Prevention Checklist
**File Location:** `task-5-cybercrime/prevention-checklist.md`

### 🛡️ Stay Safe Online: Student Checklist

1. **PIN is for GIVING, not GETTING:** Never enter your UPI PIN or scan a QR code if someone claims they are sending you money.
2. **Verify the Name:** Always double-check the "Verified Name" that appears on the UPI app before hitting the pay button.
3. **Limit Transaction Limits:** Set a daily UPI transaction limit in your bank app to minimize potential losses.
4. **Avoid Public Wi-Fi:** Never perform financial transactions or log into banking apps while on college or cafe public Wi-Fi.
5. **Enable Biometric Locks:** Always use a fingerprint or Face ID lock for your payment apps (GPay, PhonePe, Paytm) in addition to the UPI PIN.
6. **Check the 'HTTPS':** Before entering card details on a website, ensure the URL starts with `https://` and has a padlock icon.
7. **Identify Phishing:** Be wary of SMS alerts claiming your "Bank Account/KYC will be blocked." Banks never send such links via SMS.
8. **Secure Your SIM:** Enable a SIM Card PIN to prevent scammers from using your phone number for OTPs if your phone is stolen.

### 🚨 How to Report
If you are a victim of cybercrime, act within the **"Golden Hour"** (the first 2 hours):
* **Call:** 1930 (National Cyber Crime Helpline)
* **Visit:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in)

## Part C: Personal Reflection

**What surprised me most?** During my research, I was shocked to learn how scammers use "Social Engineering" to bypass high-tech security. I always thought cybercrime was about hacking code, but it is actually about hacking *people*. The fact that many victims are tech-savvy students who simply get distracted by the "urgency" of a situation was a huge eye-opener.

**My Habit Change:** As a result of this task, I have decided to stop using the same password for my university portal and my primary Gmail account. I have now started using a dedicated **Password Manager** and enabled **App-based 2FA** (like Google Authenticator) instead of relying on SMS OTPs, which can be intercepted through SIM swapping.
